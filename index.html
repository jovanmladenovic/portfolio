About

Design at the edge
of craft
and strategy.

Architecture taught me that structure is a design decision. How you organise the parts shapes what the whole becomes. I carried that into product design and have never looked back.

For more than 15 years I have led design at the intersection of user needs, business strategy, and technical constraint. Across wellbeing, healthcare, fintech, and consumer platforms, the work has always been the same: helping teams see clearly, decide well, and ship things that matter.

Today I prototype with AI before I wireframe. I ask the hard questions early. I stay close to product and engineering until the work ships. And I care deeply about the gap between what we design and what users actually experience.

How I Work

A product-minded
approach to design

01

I think before I design

Design is a collaborative problem-solving process that connects user needs, business goals, and technical realities. Discovery and framing come first, always - the shape of the question determines the quality of the answer.

02

I prototype to learn

I prototype with AI before I wireframe. Working in ambiguity is a skill, not a liability. I ask the right questions early and help teams align around a clear product vision before a single pixel is committed.

03

I design systems, not screens

Architecture taught me that scalable patterns outlast any single deliverable. I care about the whole - how components behave, how teams understand and extend the work, how design holds up over time.
