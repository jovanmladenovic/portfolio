01

Context

Tempest is a greenfield staffing platform designed for temporary workforces. Two distinct user groups with very different needs: employees who need a clear, frictionless mobile experience, and employers who need full visibility and control via a web portal. Everything — product, brand, and design system — was built from scratch.

02

Challenge

Complex scheduling logic — shifts, documents, compliance, approvals, multi-location management. Employees face fragmented processes across disconnected tools; employers lack real-time visibility into workforce status. The goal: one coherent product that feels genuinely simple for both sides of the equation.

03

Approach

Radical simplicity as the guiding principle. A familiar interaction model with no learning curve. An open, readable visual language built for diverse user types. A single design system spanning the mobile app and web portal. Every decision made to reduce friction and increase clarity — for the shift worker and the site manager alike.

The Project

An Interface
as Familiar as
a Chat App

Design started with user flows before any screens were drawn. Wireframes were reworked iteratively to surface missing features and edge cases before committing to visual design. The web portal and mobile app were developed in parallel, ensuring consistency at every interaction layer.

The approach centred on openness and friendliness — a color palette and visual language that felt immediately readable for a diverse, non-technical user base. A custom icon set was designed from scratch: shift requests, documents, messages, shift swaps, sick leave, holidays — each visually distinct yet tonally unified. The experience flows naturally from login through worker profile, home feed, shift detail, and calendar — with no dead ends and no confusion.

Impact

What Tempest Delivered

A complete end-to-end product spanning branding, mobile app, and web portal — built from zero to a fully functional, production-ready design. Two very different user experiences unified under one coherent design system, with a visual identity strong enough to stand on its own.

Learning

What This Project Taught Me

A strong conceptual anchor early on is critical. The "chat card" pattern kept the design consistent as the product grew in scope. Designing for clarity rather than sophistication travels further when the user base is diverse and the tasks are time-sensitive. Simplicity is not a limitation — it is the product.

Takeaway

Design That Works

The best products for complex industries are not the most complicated ones. When design gets out of the way, it becomes invisible — and that invisibility is the point. Tempest proved that a thoughtful interaction model and a clean visual language are enough to make even the most operationally complex workflow feel effortless.
